The one thing to take from this page

The signals that are cheap and legally clean (owner age proxy, succession gap, trajectory, UCC, filings) are the ones we've tested, and they did not predict sales in two blind backtests. The signals the plan rates highest predictive value (prior sale, prior listing, competitor acquired, key-man departure) are untested and depend on the legally contested sources (LinkedIn/BizBuySell/Glassdoor scraping, FINRA). So the proven-cheap column is weak, and the promising column is unverified on both law and prediction. That gap is the product's central open question.

Update, Jul 1 2026: Part A behavioral obtainability closed (PASS)

Two 25-firm obtainability runs closed the last Phase 0 Part A exit criterion (SEC run: 15 labeled + 10 AZ firms; web run: a different 25-firm labeled sample, 12 sellers / 13 controls; the pre-sale and relocation figures below are from the web sample). Four Category-C signal types are obtainable without heroics from SEC/IAPD alone, provenance-clean: headcount/AUM decline (Item 5), advisor-departure counts (Item 5.B/IAPD diffs, the SEC substitute for U5), office closures (Schedule D), and ownership/succession events (Schedule A/B). The open web added little that is both allowed and forward-looking: exit/deal language is post-hoc (pre-sale-visible 1/12), SOS events work only where an open-data API exists (CO yes, NV walled), home/relocation is not obtainable from allowed sources (0/25), and Wayback decay is still untested (sandbox-blocked, re-run pending). Obtainable ≠ predictive: none of these lanes has been prediction-tested. Details: SEC_OBTAINABILITY.md, WEB_OBTAINABILITY.md, closure memo.

Owner-age addendum (same day): a 15-principal true-age diagnosis suggests real ages DO separate sellers from non-sellers (AUC 0.68, small sample) while the free ADV-derived estimate does not: the free proxy, not the thesis, appears to be the weak link. Confirming this requires licensed date-of-birth data (~$0.02/record plus vendor minimums). The POC is built on free public data only, so this stops here by design: the licensed re-test is the first item on a production budget, not part of the demo. (Diagnosis also leaned on voter-roll birth years, which are use-restricted in many states; counsel item.)

Reading the "Predictive" column: "Tested ✓" means it ran through the Part B blind backtests. "Untested" means no validation exists yet. Note this is different from the plan's Confidence Grade, which measures evidence certainty, not whether the signal predicts a sale. A signal can be Confidence-A (verified to source) and still Untested for prediction.

How to use this with the client

Lead with the demo (the capability is real and impressive). Hand this map alongside it as the honest "path to production": the green rows are shippable today, the amber/red rows are a licensing-and-legal budget line, and the predictive column is the candid statement that the model's bets still need a proper behavioral backtest. That framing sells judgment, not just a screenshot.
